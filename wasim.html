The Brief

Critical infrastructure systems — power grids, water networks, transport — fail silently. Operators rely on fragmented dashboards, delayed alerts, and manual inspections that can't scale to a modern city.

"How do you give a field operator AI-grade situational awareness in the palm of their hand — without overwhelming them?"

CBI4AI is a mobile interface concept that brings together 3D city-scale visualisation, real-time AI predictions, and a voice-first interaction model. The design challenge: make the complex feel immediate, and the dangerous feel clear.

The prototype explores three core modes — Map, Predict, and Failure Zones — moving between a bird's-eye network view and a granular street-level analysis.

Design Decisions

01
3D as a data layer
The city isn't decoration — it's the data. Switching between 2D overhead and 3D isometric gives operators two mental models: network topology and physical reality. Both are necessary.
02
Neon on black = signal, not style
High-contrast neon on near-black reduces cognitive load in field conditions (bright sunlight, night shifts). Cyan for navigation, magenta for active states, red for critical alerts — each hue carries semantic meaning.
03
Voice as the primary modality
When operators are wearing gloves or managing physical systems, touch is unreliable. The microphone button anchors every screen — the interface is designed to be operated without looking.
